"He drifted off into sleep and Janie looked down on him and felt a self-crushing love. So her soul crawled out from its hiding place" - Page 171 (PDF)

The image seems to contain a human head that is lying on the grass. The human seems to be sleeping, referencing the idea that "He drifted off into sleep." We can also see a reflection of the human's face on the grass. Perhaps, this is Janie's soul - a reflection of her mind and heart. It is coming out of her body to love once again: "Her soul crawled out from its hiding place. A soul is what provides one’s ability to love and to have feelings. The usage of the word “crawl” demonstrates how difficult love is, how we sometimes enter it reluctantly, slowly, almost against our will. The illuminated soul suggests that Janie has found happiness with Tea Cake. The soul is not the only lit-up part of the image, however. The grass is also starting to become green. In contrast to Image 3, the grass is much more alive. This alludes to the idea that Janie's love is not dead and has been rejuvenated once again.
