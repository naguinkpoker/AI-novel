This image contains lit-up grass surrounding a dark hole. This quote refers to the idea that Janie's marriage with Jody Starks was now unhappy as well. The same passion and desire that had captivated their marriage in the beginning no longer existed. The white end at the top of the hole is, perhaps, the tail of a wedding dress. Everything outside the dark hole was brightly lit-up and had vibrancy. Perhaps, Janie and Jody's marriage was now this closed, restricted relationship that had now lacked passion and happiness; it had been sucked into a void. Her marriage lacked light, which, as established previously, is referred to as happiness.
